# Showcase capsule: sequences-abstract-ad

Tags: abstract storytelling, semantic morph, constant motion, audio-led, density arc, light/dark inversion.

Use when the film is motion-first, abstract, music-led, morph-heavy, or needs one persistent entity to change meaning continuously across product states.

## Transferable lessons

- Give one object semantic continuity: caret, prompt seed, keyframe, playhead, player control, and final punctuation are successive roles of the same entity.
- Map macro boundaries to the soundtrack and reserve rapid multiplication for the musical flurry instead of running every beat at maximum density.
- Keep readable copy settled while the camera or environment moves, then resolve accumulated density through subtraction.
- Use simple 2D camera travel, portals, paths, and a single light-to-midnight inversion to create scale without complex 3D.

## Known mistakes to avoid

- Do not substitute decorative particles, unseeded randomness, or generic 3D for a causal transformation.
- Do not cut between unrelated visual worlds or give nested layers competing camera ownership.
- Do not spread accent color across the full film; save it for the meaningful peak.
- Do not repeat the abstract film's exact prompt-seed story or final line.
