# Make your prompt move — production plan

## Status and boundary

This is the durable source of truth for the abstract Sequences launch film.
The final film must not be assembled until the component review sheet has been
approved. Current scope is preproduction plus reusable component authoring.

- Target: 1920×1080, 30 fps, 30.333 seconds / 910 frames.
- Soundtrack: `audio/music/cinematic_fast_violin.mp3` in full.
- Narration: none.
- Product hierarchy: ChatGPT is the originating spark; Sequences is the main
  character and turns the prompt into a storyboard, timeline, and launch film.
- Working title and final promise: **Make your prompt move.**

## Creative thesis

A single prompt becomes a moving system. A real, restrained ChatGPT composer
creates the spark. One black caret-dot escapes the interface, passes through the
canonical ChatGPT knot, and becomes the persistent green prompt seed. Sequences
then converts that seed into ordered scenes, a directed timeline, and a playable
launch film. The film stays abstract, calm, strange, and continuously alive.

The visual storytelling is semantic rather than decorative: every transformation
changes the meaning of the same object. The dot is a caret, send action, keyframe,
playhead, player control, and final period. The camera follows that continuity.

## Reference analysis

### `abstract_openai_ad.mp4`

The OpenAI film uses one black dot as cursor, particle, construction point,
portal, and brand atom. It repeatedly performs semantic morphs—dot to text, text
to geometry, geometry to field, field to brand—rather than cutting between
unrelated cards. Roughly four fifths of the sampled frames are near-white, so
color remains consequential. Giant type becomes physical scenery; construction
guides, grids, and measurements give the abstraction purpose. Density accumulates
into a collage and then resolves through subtraction. The camera follows the
transformations instead of observing them from a fixed distance.

Borrow:

- the dot language and deterministic particle lattice;
- oversized glyphs used as camera framing;
- sparse construction geometry and one brief palette burst;
- collage accumulation followed by a severe final reduction;
- a canonical knot reveal, never an approximate six-capsule mark.

Avoid:

- the long font-history/specimen passage;
- photo collage and glyph spam that do not prove Sequences;
- treating the OpenAI aesthetic as a surface skin without a causal story.

### `abstract_motion_graphics_example.mp4`

The second film makes objects become entrances to the next world: screen to
landscape, clock to portal, icon to scene. Constant movement comes from 2D camera
pans, pushes, slight rotations, parallax, and environment drift—not difficult 3D.
Circles, radial rays, rounded rectangles, and flat planes create spatial
continuity. White-to-midnight inversions reset energy. Off-center framing and
foreground shapes keep simple scenes dimensional, while held copy stays alive
because the world around it keeps moving.

Borrow:

- portal transitions built from circles and rounded frames;
- 2D camera grammar, off-center compositions, and gentle parallax;
- a single light-to-midnight inversion at the musical peak;
- environmental drift during readable holds.

Avoid:

- characters, city scenes, vehicles, and complex perspective construction;
- illustrative worlds that distract from the prompt-to-film transformation.

The curated proof frames and exact takeaways live in `reference/README.md`.

## Music analysis

The full track is 30.3314 seconds, 44.1 kHz stereo MP3. The working grid is
approximately 124 BPM in 4/4:

- quarter note: 0.483871 s;
- eighth note: 0.241935 s;
- bar: 1.935484 s;
- audible pickup: about 0.534 s;
- first complete downbeat: about 2.217 s;
- final musical hit: about 29.315 s;
- integrated loudness: about −23 LUFS;
- loudness range: about 5.8 LU;
- true peak: about −7.9 dBFS;
- tonal center: close to C, with deliberately ambiguous mode.

The sensation of speed comes from repeated eighth-note attacks, not constant
high loudness. The 21.572–27.379 passage is lower in RMS but highest in transient
density, so it drives multiplication and rearrangement rather than a generic
brightness pulse.

## Story: six macro beats

### 1. The ask — 0.000–2.217

A minimal, real ChatGPT composer sits on warm white. The exact knot is aligned to
the input rail. `Make the launch feel alive.` types deterministically while a
black caret-dot follows the text.

### 2. The handoff — 2.217–6.088

The send control compresses. The camera dives into the black dot. The canonical
ChatGPT knot unfurls, then releases one green prompt seed toward a distant
Sequences ring. ChatGPT is visibly the source; Sequences is the destination by
the end of the beat.

### 3. The sequence — 6.088–11.895

The seed crosses into the Sequences world. A particle lattice reorganizes into an
oversized `S`, timeline rails, and five scene nodes. `A prompt.` physically
rearranges into `A sequence.`

### 4. The build — 11.895–17.701

The camera travels laterally through five storyboard frames, each containing a
different moving micro-scene. The original prompt stays visible in a large
Sequences prompt rail. A single green playhead connects and orders the frames.

### 5. The result — 17.701–27.379

The selected frame expands into a playable launch film. At 21.572 the world
inverts from white to midnight. At 23.508 the result bursts into a measured
OpenAI-inspired collage of frames, dots, glyphs, and paths. By 26.411, all of it
converges into one polished player.

### 6. The lockup — 27.379–30.333

The player becomes a rounded rectangle, then a line, then the final period after
`Sequences`. The wordmark settles by the final hit at 29.315. A small exact
ChatGPT knot and prompt-seed trail remain as provenance. The tagline reads
`Make your prompt move.`

## Design system

### Palette

- warm field — `#F7F7F3`
- paper — `#FFFFFF`
- ink — `#11110F`
- guide — `#D8D8D0`
- Sequences green — `#10A37F`
- sky — `#7ECFF8`
- lilac — `#B9ADEB`
- butter — `#F4D96A`
- cobalt — `#2457D6`
- midnight — `#122B4A`

Target ratio is roughly 80% white/ink, 12% green, and 8% spectral accents. The
spectral colors appear only at the peak.

### Typography

Montserrat 500–600 is the restrained product grotesque. IBM Plex Mono 500 is
reserved for construction labels, scene indices, and time. Hero type ranges from
140–240 px. Product UI ranges from 28–42 px.

### Geometry

The recurring vocabulary is circles, rings, hairlines, giant glyphs, rounded film
frames, and one green connective path. There is no glass, holographic interface,
generic particle noise, or complex 3D.

## Motion contract

Primary verbs: **orbit, unfurl, dock**.

- One prompt seed persists from caret through final period.
- At every moment at least one of seed, camera, or environment moves.
- The camera owns only 2D translate, uniform scale, and slight rotation.
- Children own local motion. No competing nested camera transforms.
- Shapes are destinations and transition masks, not decorative overlays.
- Readable type holds while non-text environment motion continues.
- Eighth-note cuts/multiplication are reserved for 21.572–27.379.
- Background rings may react 3–12%; text never pulses.
- The final tail has one nearly invisible 1.014-second breath.
- No layout-property animation, timers, CSS runtime animation, unseeded random,
  network media, or infinite loops.

## Reusable component inventory

1. **ChatGPT composer** — empty, typing, submitted.
2. **Canonical knot** — rose sprite unfurl to exact SVG rest.
3. **Prompt seed** — caret, send dot, keyframe, playhead, player control, period.
4. **Deterministic dot field** — sphere, `S`, timeline, collage, collapse.
5. **Shape portal** — dot, ring, rounded frame, full-screen viewport.
6. **Sequences prompt rail** — prompt, building, storyboard ready.
7. **Storyboard strip** — five distinct launch-film frames with internal motion.
8. **Sequence timeline** — nodes, connective path, playhead, verified endpoint.
9. **Result player** — selected frame, expanded player, active micro-film.
10. **Glyph portal** — oversized `S` as camera tunnel and framing device.
11. **Collage field** — eighth-note accumulation and measured convergence.
12. **Final lockup** — wordmark, tagline, small ChatGPT provenance mark.
13. **Camera world** — one persistent transform owner demonstrating parallax.

All components are native SVG/DOM using fixed coordinate arrays. Image generation
is intentionally excluded from the first component pass.

## Production gates

### Gate 1 — story and design

This document, the machine-readable design/component/sequence files, and the
curated reference packet preserve the approved direction.

### Gate 2 — components

The 26-second component lab presents one component every two seconds. Proof frames
are captured at 1, 3, 5, …, 25 seconds and combined into a 4×4 master review
sheet plus smaller thematic strips. User feedback happens here.

### Gate 3 — full film

Only after component approval, assemble the 30.333-second film against the exact
musical grid, run strict HyperFrames verification, render, mux the violin track,
inspect the finished film, and revise from user feedback.
