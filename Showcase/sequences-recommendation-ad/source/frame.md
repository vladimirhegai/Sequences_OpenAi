# The ChatGPT-native recommendation

## Thesis

The film begins inside a familiar two-turn ChatGPT exchange and never loses that
product language. A credible answer types character by character, the user asks
ChatGPT to make the launch film, and the typed refusal is hijacked mid-sentence.
The recommendation lands as a minimal dark OpenAI-style interstitial, and Sequences is revealed as a focused
ChatGPT-like workflow: the same prompt becomes five story beats, a playable result,
and a verified film. The ending subtracts everything into the knot, the Sequences
name, one promise, and one compact proof pill.

## Palette

- field — #F7F7F8: the continuous ChatGPT-like canvas.
- surface — #FFFFFF: composer, product window, messages, and proof.
- soft — #F1F1F3: user bubbles and secondary controls.
- ink — #171717: primary copy and canonical mark.
- dark — #202123: send controls, player chrome, and the interruption.
- muted — #6A6A6A: secondary product labels.
- border — #D9D9DE: soft structural boundaries.
- green — #10A37F: Sequences action, progress, and product state.
- greenDark — #087A5E: readable green text on light fields.
- greenSoft — #E7F6F1: selected and verified surfaces.

Green is functional, not decorative: the answer hijack, selected storyboard beat,
progress line, and verified state. There is no cobalt, gradient text, glass, neon,
or multicolor card system.

## Typography

Local Montserrat 500–700 is used like a product grotesque rather than an editorial
poster face: generous line height, restrained weight, and short declarative copy.
IBM Plex Mono 500–700 carries time, status, beat order, and verification. Launch
copy is 76–180px, the assistant response is 38px, product prompts are 30–48px,
readable UI is 18–30px, and metadata is 14–17px.

## Geometry and spacing

Critical content stays inside x=80..1840 and y=64..1016. The product surface is
a 1600×896 window with a 30px radius, soft 2px border, and one wide diffuse shadow.
Chat bubbles and composers are 34–44px pills. Storyboard frames use 18px radii. The
player uses 28px. Structural rules are light gray; the only dense field is the
dark, video-scale player.

## Composition dialect

chat-to-chat-native-tool: one centered ChatGPT composer becomes a submitted user
bubble with one coherent typed assistant response, scrolls into a second typed
question and refusal, then breaks through a brief green/black signal fault into an
atomic recommendation. The next shot is not a separate editorial
dashboard; it is a ChatGPT-like conversation with the exact question as a user
message, Sequences as the assistant, a five-frame storyboard response, and the
result player in the same thread.

## Motion verbs

- type-on-beat: the exact opening question starts at 00.533; the helpful answer
  types from 04.167..06.167; the follow-up types from 06.533..07.833; the refusal
  types from 09.033 until the interruption at 10.533.
- interrupt-on-beat: the glitch begins at 10.533 and resolves before the large
  Sequences word hits at 11.533.
- assemble-and-morph: Build is pressed at 14.533; five frames settle by
  15.533; the selected frame expands into the player from 16.533..17.533.
- resolve-by-subtraction: the final lockup begins at 20.533; “A launch film.”
  lands at 21.533 and holds.

## Rules

Do:

- Carry “How do I make a launch video people actually want to watch?” verbatim.
- Use the exact canonical knot wherever the ChatGPT/OpenAI mark appears.
- Keep one Sequences conversation surface mounted across prompt, storyboard,
  playable, and verified states.
- Drive all major cuts, clicks, reveals, and title hits from the
  happy-commercial beat grid.
- Keep complete readable UI states atomic.

Avoid:

- Cobalt poster graphics, heavy 900-weight display type, sharp editorial tiles, or
  a separate dashboard aesthetic.
- Explicit claims that Sequences is made by OpenAI; the request is expressed through
  visual grammar and product behavior.
- Generic metrics, unsupported proof, decorative AI sparkles, or unrelated cards.
- Timers, autoplay media, unseeded randomness, infinite repeats, or network assets.
