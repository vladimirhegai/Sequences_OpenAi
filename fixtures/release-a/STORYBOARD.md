# Release A storyboard

Format: 1920×1080 · 15 seconds · silent · three modular Hyperframes compositions.

## Story direction

This film tells product teams that Sequences turns approved evidence into an editable release story whose AI changes can be inspected before they are accepted.

| Time | Scene | Focal event | Proof |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0.0–4.6s | Evidence intake | Owned screenshot, approved claim, and unsupported claim enter as distinct records. | Evidence has status, dimensions, hash, and human approval. |
| 4.6–10.8s | Compose workspace | A selected UI card keeps its identity while moving from “14 tabs” to “1 story.” | Viewer, real timeline language, stable `hfId`, source file, and interval stay visible. |
| 10.8–15.0s | Verified receipt | The candidate receipt resolves line by line, then holds on Apply or Reject. | Strict check, scene scope, unchanged-scene proof, and inverse patch are inspectable. |

## Asset audit

No external brand, screenshot, music, logo, or font file is required. All visual evidence is fictional and drawn as owned HTML/CSS. Runtime and GSAP files are copied from the exact locked dependencies during bootstrap.
